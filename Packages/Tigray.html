Aksum Monument

The Aksumite Civilization in the ancient city of Aksum, located in modern-day Ethiopia, is known for its impressive monuments, particularly the monolithic obelisks.These obelisks, dating from the 3rd and 4th centuries AD, are unique masterpieces of human creativity Carved mainly from solid blocks of nepheline syenite, these obelisks are believed to have come from quarries several miles west of Aksum. The tallest stelae likely commemorated royalty, while smaller ones were probably commissioned by local elites.

Aksum Tsion

Aksum Tsion, also known as Maryam Tsion, is a sacred site within the Ethiopian OrthodoxTewahido Church, located in the Tigray region of Northern Ethiopia. This church is considered one of the oldest in Ethiopia, tracing back to the time of Christianity's introduction in the country. Emperor Fasiledes rebuilt the church after it was destroyed by Ahmad ibn Ibrahim al-Ghazi in the 16th century, and Emperor Haile Selassie constructed the elaborate current church structure in the 1950s.

Al Nejashi Mosque

The Al Nejashi Mosque is located in Negash, Tigray, Ethiopia. It is believed to be Africa's first mosque, established after 615 CE when the first Muslims migrated to Abyssinia during the reign of Najashi, after fleeing persecution in Mecca.The mosque was named after Najashi and has historical significance as a place of refuge for early Muslims. In 2018, the mosque underwent renovation with funding from the Turkish Cooperation and Coordination Agency.

Debre Damo

Debre Damo is a monastery located on an isolated mountain in the northern part of Tigray, Ethiopia. It was built in the sixth century AD and is unique compared to most Ethiopian monasteries. Debre Damo is only accessible by climbing up by a rope, which is made of "plaited leather," lowered from the cliffs, which visitors tie around their waist and are then pulled up by a monk at the top of the cliffs. It is only accessible to men and male animals.

Abune Yemata

Abuna Yemata Guh is a monolithic church located in the Hawzen woreda of the Tigray Region, Ethiopia. Situated at a height of 2,580 meters (8,460 feet), this church is known for its challenging yet rewarding climb and its historical significance. Carved into the side of a tall sandstone spire in the Gheralta area, the church dates back to the 5th century and is dedicated to Abuna Yemata, one of the Nine Saints who played a crucial role in spreading Christianity in Ethiopia.
